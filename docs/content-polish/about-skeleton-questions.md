## Proposed skeleton (EN version)

The current About is 4 short sections (Who I am / What I write about / Professional / Why English / Contact). I'm proposing a slightly longer 6-section structure — still scannable, but with more dimension.

### 1. Who I am (one paragraph, ~60 words)
A single dense paragraph. Three or four sentences. Not a CV. Things like: name, where I live, where I'm from, what I do with my days, what I'm reading. Voice over completeness.

### 2. What this site is (one paragraph, ~60 words)
Two ideas: (a) it's a record of thinking and living — both, on purpose; (b) most new writing goes to Substack first, only the pieces that I want to keep close come home. Frames the curation philosophy without sounding precious.

### 3. What I'm thinking about right now (3–5 short bullets or one short paragraph, ~80 words)
A genuinely *current* snapshot. Updates every 6–12 months, signed with a date. This section is what makes the page feel alive vs. archival. Things like: current reading project, current life shift, current open question. The opposite of an evergreen bio.

### 4. The longer arc (1–2 paragraphs, ~140 words)
Where you came from intellectually and where you're going. The Henan village → Tsinghua → Ele.me → BQ → GLP → independent quant → VU AI → Amsterdam arc, but selectively, and connected to *why* — not as a CV walkthrough. End with the current center of gravity (Kantian moral philosophy + active love + Cloud Brain).

### 5. What I value (3–5 lines, prose or aphorisms — your call)
The Kantian core stated plainly, but in your own words. Freedom, integrity, truth, dignity, love — but rendered in concrete sentences rather than a list. This section earns the tagline `Plain living, high thinking.` if it's not florid.

### 6. Contact / where else to find me (existing format)
Email, GitHub, LinkedIn, Substack. Same as today, last item.

EN says "the site is in English because the audience I'm speaking to is international" without apology. ZH version says "中文写作仍在 Substack 继续" without apology either.
